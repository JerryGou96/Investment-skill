# Rotation Map Output Template

Use the sections below unless the user's requested scope is narrower. Omit empty sections rather than padding the answer.

## Opening conclusion

Write one paragraph answering:

- Is this broad risk-on/risk-off, cyclical broadening, defensive rotation, or localized de-risking?
- Where is money moving from and to?
- Is the evidence one-day, tactical, or trend-level?

## 1. Market regime

Present a compact table with broad, equal-weight, small-cap, growth/value, volatility, rates, and credit observations. Include the spread between equal-weight and capitalization-weighted performance when useful.

End with:

- Regime label
- Confidence: high, medium, or low
- Evidence horizon: intraday, 1-day, 5-day, 20-day, or 60-day

## 2. Traditional-sector rotation

Show the strongest and weakest sectors with:

| Sector | Proxy | 1D | 5D | 20D relative to SPY | State |

Use `N/A — insufficient verified history` for missing periods.

Explain whether leadership is defensive, cyclical, rate-sensitive, or growth-driven.

## 3. AI-internal rotation map

Show:

| AI sub-sector | Representative basket | Equal-weight 1D | 5D | 20D relative | Breadth | State |

Cover at least compute, foundry/packaging, semiconductor equipment, memory, optical connectivity, networking, power/cooling, cloud/neocloud, and software/applications when relevant. Add PCB/interconnect, systems, energy, or robotics when material.

Always give semiconductor equipment its own row. Do not combine it with foundry/packaging. When the data permits, compare the liquid core equipment basket (verified ASML, AMAT, LRCX and KLAC) with advanced-packaging inspection/test names, and state whether capex, bookings or backlog confirm the price move.

For the three most important groups, explain:

- What price is saying
- Whether fundamentals confirm
- Whether leaders and second-line names agree
- What would confirm or invalidate the move

## 4. Breadth and participation

Report available advancers/decliners, new highs/lows, equal-weight versus cap-weight, moving-average breadth, median return, and volume. State explicitly whether breadth confirms the headline index move.

## 5. Price versus fundamentals

Use the price/fundamental matrix. Highlight:

- Strong fundamentals but weak price
- Strong price without revisions
- Confirmed deterioration
- Potential unpriced improvement

Do not infer revisions from price alone.

## 6. Momentum change

Identify the largest verified 5-day relative-strength changes and whether the 1-day move agrees with the 20-day trend. If history is missing, say so and omit rankings.

## 7. Catalysts

Separate:

- Today's confirmed drivers
- Next-session to two-week catalysts
- One-to-two-quarter fundamental milestones

Use exact dates only when verified. Separate official facts from reported explanations and analyst inference.

## 8. Confirmation checklist

Provide three to five observable signals, such as:

- A benchmark holding or losing a verified session level
- Equal-weight/cap-weight relative performance
- AI sub-basket performance versus SMH, QQQ, or SPY
- Breadth above a threshold
- A scheduled earnings or macro release

Avoid arbitrary technical levels. Label session highs/lows as session highs/lows, not support or resistance unless technically validated.

## 9. Rotation verdict

Summarize in a table:

| Area | State | Evidence | Main risk |

End with a concise verdict containing:

1. Current phase
2. Best-confirmed relative strength
3. Weakest or most crowded area
4. What evidence would change the conclusion

## Citation and data note

State the market date, cutoff, and quote status. Place source links near factual claims. If after-hours prices are used, separate them from official closes.
